[ ABOUT // 01 ]

I didn't start in design.

I started in computer science, spent four years thinking in systems and code, then moved into a two-year M.Des program. That shift taught me something important: the best design thinking comes from understanding how things actually work underneath.

[ ABOUT // 02 ]

How people navigate uncertainty.

Most of my interest in UX comes from watching how people make decisions when the information isn't complete. That's more interesting than interface polish.

[ ABOUT // 03 ]

Brand and product are the same problem.

Now I work across brand and product because I think they're the same problem. A packaging decision and a checkout flow are both answering the same question: does this person trust us enough to take the next step? I'm most useful in the room where those two conversations happen at the same time.
