(WHY I DESIGN) — 01 / 02

Curiosity led me to design.
Systems made me stay.

[ NOTE 01 // SYSTEMS ]

Thinking in Systems

I started in computer science, where I learned to think in systems before I ever designed an interface. That mindset taught me to look beyond individual screens and understand how every decision shapes a larger experience.

[ NOTE 02 // BEHAVIOR ]

UX Discovery & Psychology

Even before design, I was fascinated by human psychology and how visuals, media, and everyday experiences influence our decisions.

That curiosity led me to UX, where I discovered that great design isn't built on assumptions, but on observing behavior. People often say one thing and do another, and understanding that gap is where meaningful design begins.

[ NOTE 03 // COHERENCE ]

Brand + Product Coherence

Over time, I realized a brand isn't just a logo and a product isn't just a collection of screens. Together, they shape how people understand, trust, and remember something.

That's why my work spans brand identity, product design, and storytelling. I'm interested in designing complete experiences, not isolated deliverables.

[ NOTE 04 // LONGEVITY ]

Friction & Inevitability

I'm less interested in adding features than removing friction. Less interested in making things look different than making them feel inevitable.

Long term, I want to build products, brands, and eventually companies that people genuinely remember.
